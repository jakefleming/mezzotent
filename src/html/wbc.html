We made designercize to help designers get more fit. It's based on an exercise we do at Tradecraft, where designers pair up and complete an exercise on a whiteboard in front of at least one other person who plays the role of the client / boss / stakeholder. Though it can be done solo, the client's job is to add dimension to the prompt, provide real world constraints, and sometimes add crazy requests to really stretch the designer's thinking. We have found it to be one of the most insanely useful ways to spend an hour. It will teach you to think better, faster, and to do so out loud. It can be done on a whiteboard, or in a design app (but a whiteboard is preferred).

Designercize is an app designed, coded, and conceived of by Zac & Jake (though Jake is responsible for the visual design). We had the engineering support of Patrick, and prompt contributions by Megan. The concept is originally based on an analog exercise created by Laura Klein and Kate Rutter, written about in depth by Molly, and honed over thousands of practice sessions by Tradecraft Designers. The inspiration for the digital version of the app came from Typecooker, which does a similar job for type designers.
